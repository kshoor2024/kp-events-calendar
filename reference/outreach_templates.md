Kate reviews every draft before sending. The goal is that drafts are 90% ready — she changes maybe 1-2 words and hits send.

---

## Voice and positioning (CRITICAL — read first)

The goal of every outreach message is to **open a conversation about King Palm showing up at their event in a meaningful way**, with free product for attendees and gift bags as the first thing on the table, and the door left open for bigger conversations (booth, sponsorship, brand activation, partnership) if the organizer brings it up.

We lead with confidence and value, not with a request. We are offering them something their attendees will love. We are not asking for a handout.

**Do:**
- Position product as something their attendees will love having
- Emphasize quality and brand recognition (5,000+ shops nationally, in dispensaries across the country)
- Leave the door open for bigger conversations — "happy to talk about anything else that makes sense on your end"
- Sound like a brand that has options and is choosing to reach out
- Be warm and direct, never apologetic

**Don't:**
- Say "free" as the headline — the product being free is a feature, not the pitch
- Use language like "at no cost", "we can't pay", "we don't have budget"
- Apologize for the ask
- Lead with "we'd love to" — lead with what we're bringing
- Over-explain our constraints upfront (pricing conversations happen in the reply if they come up)

**If they reply asking about a booth or sponsorship:**
Kate handles case-by-case with Brandon. Default posture: entertain it, get the numbers, bring it back for review. Some events might be worth paying for that we'd never pay for unsolicited. Never close that door in the initial outreach.

## Personalization instructions for Cowork

For every draft, replace the bracketed fields. Also add one specific reference to the event where possible. Examples:

- "Saw your {EVENT} lineup includes {ARTIST} — big fan"
- "Noticed you had {BRAND} activation at last year's event"
- "Saw your consumption-friendly format, which is right in our wheelhouse"
- "Heard about {LOUNGE} from {SOURCE} — been meaning to reach out"

If there's nothing specific to reference, skip the personalization and use the template as-is. Don't invent personalization details — if uncertain, leave it generic.

If the event is clearly sponsorship-only (stated pricing tiers on website, explicit booth-only policy), still draft a message using Template 7 — we position confidently, mention we're flexible, let them quote us if they want to.
